Axolotl

Mexican axolotl salamanders (Ambystoma mexicanum) are amphibians that spend their whole lives underwater. They exist in the wild in only one place - the lake complex of Xochimilco.

I had three axolotls - two wild type ones named Penny and Peanut and a leucistic one named Mrs Vanilla. Wild type axolotls are dark grayish green with black and olive mottling. Leucistic axolotls have a translucent white body, pink gills and dark eyes. They often get mistaken for the albino type, but those don't have any pigments and their eyes are red.

Unfortunately the two wild type axolotls passed away in the last years, so the only one left is the leucistic axolotl, but it will get two new companions soon.
